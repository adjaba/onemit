Many generations, one community

MIT’s Great Dome has come to symbolize our community of faculty, students, staff, and alumni. Now these individuals have lent their names to embody the Great Dome.

The image in this exhibit incorporates the names of every member of the MIT community from our founding in 1861 until the first semester of 2018—more than 270,000 individuals in all! The carefully arranged names have been etched into the surface of the wafer, with each letter functioning like a light or dark pixel. The result: generation after generation coming together to represent the shared history, character, and sense of purpose that unites us.

We call the project “One.MIT.” It’s an apt name for an exhibit in MIT.nano, which serves every department and any researcher who needs its tools. Use this website to search for a name and its location on the wafer.
